Coffee painting for my Art Appreciation class. Hare's the cringe ass rationale made for it;

> *I am a spectator of my own life.* I keep my mouth shout and my head tilted down. This world does not need me, so I've opt to escape into my own. Which is why I'm incredibly found of creating original characters (OCs). Everything I do is for the sake of them. Attending social events for character studies, researching about an OC's field of interests, and perusing profitable careers to have more free time dedicated for making art about them. Nothing done is ever truly an act by "[REDACTED]". All my actions are just a means to develop my OCs. They're kind of like my shadows, as depicted in my illustration. They shift and morph to create illusions of grandeur. They're the tiny bit of hope that gives me a reason to wake up another day and have the courage to see it through. But when the sun set and the lights dim, the fact remains; Those illusions will stay as one. This painting  is a reminder that the fiction I make is still a product of my own hand. I have the ability to become *more* than a spectator.
